Built for the World Cup

AllOY26 is not a generic betting tracker. It is a tournament-focused analytics workspace built around World Cup matches, groups, knockout stages, markets, strategies and Betfair CSV history.

Import Betfair CSVs

Import settled bets or betting history from Betfair. Tested with +30k bets and +2k markets across all 104 matches. Supports the main match markets. See FAQ

Analyse every market

Review P/L, ROI, odds ranges, volume, market rankings, scatter plots and isolated per-market drilldowns.

Rebuild your work

See how every back and lay changed your exposure across selections throughout the timeline of a match.

Track the tournament

Follow groups, matchdays, knockout rounds and a user-built KO tree that creates future matches inside the app.

Compare strategies

Create strategies, assign markets automatically and compare results across P/L, ROI, win rate, drawdown and other metrics.

Anonymous

Everything runs on your computer. The app has no login, no internet connection, no Betfair connection and does not share your data.

FAQ

Is AllOY26 free?

Yes. The app is free to download and use.

Does it require an account?

No. There is no login, account system or registration.

Does the app upload my data?

No. AllOY26 is offline. Your CSVs and data stay on your computer.

Is it only for the World Cup?

Yes. AllOY26 was built specifically around the World Cup structure, calendar, groups and knockout stages.

What files does it work with?

The app was designed to import Betfair CSVs related to World Cup bets and markets.

Which Betfair markets are supported?

AllOY26 supports the main World Cup match markets:

Match Odds; Over/Under Goals; Correct Score; Half Time; First Half Goals; Half Time Score; Extra Time; ET Over/Under Goals; ET Correct Score; Both Teams to Score; Double Chance; Draw no Bet; Method of Victory; To Qualify; Winner; To Lift The Trophy.

It currently does not support corners, cards, handicaps or player markets.

Why does Windows show a warning?

Because AllOY26 is a new indie app and is not digitally code-signed yet, Windows SmartScreen may show a warning on first install. This is common for new applications from independent developers.

To install it, click "More info" and then "Run anyway". You can also verify the file integrity using the SHA256 checksum published in the Version section.
